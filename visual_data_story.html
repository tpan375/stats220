Seeing Red: Observing Red Clothing in Public Spaces

Observing Red Over Time

Bar chart showing average red sightings by hour.

In this plot we see that the most frequent item of red clothing observed in public settings were T-shirts by far, with hats being in second place. This helps us understand that when people are spotted wearing red clothing it is most likely that it is a T-shirt.

Whos Wearing Red and How?

Bar chart of red clothing types with item counts, highlighting hats.

Grouped Red Sightings

Boxplot showing distribution of red sightings by group size.

In this plot we see the relationship between the number of people wearing red observed in the last 10 minutes and how many people were in at least groups of 2. We can see that as the number of people wearing red increases the average number of people in groups increase. We also see that when there are 3 people observed wearing red in the last 10 minutes the variability of the number of people in groups increases as it has the largest variance.
